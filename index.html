CBAM Implementation Guide

+

Build systematic processes for carbon border adjustment compliance

+
+ +
+
+

🎯 Phase 1: Foundation Setup

+
+
+ 1 +
+
Establish CBAM Team & Governance
+
Create cross-functional team with legal, procurement, operations, and finance representatives. Define roles, responsibilities, and reporting structures.
+
+
+
+ 2 +
+
Map Products & Supply Chains
+
Identify all imported products within CBAM scope and trace their supply chains to determine reporting obligations.
+
+
+
+ 3 +
+
Define Compliance Strategy
+
Set data collection standards, timelines, and internal review mechanisms. Align CBAM objectives with corporate sustainability targets.
+
+
+
+
+ +
+

🤝 Phase 2: Supplier Engagement

+
+
+ 1 +
+
Supplier Onboarding
+
Notify suppliers of CBAM requirements. Provide guidance on data expectations and timeline for submissions.
+
+
+
+ 2 +
+
Collect Emissions Data
+
Use standardized templates to gather verified emissions data and production details for each product.
+
+
+
+ 3 +
+
Validate & Store Information
+
Review submissions for completeness and accuracy, then upload to secure internal systems for audit trail.
+
+
+
+
+ +
+

📊 Phase 3: Reporting & Review

+
+
+ 1 +
+
Compile CBAM Reports
+
Aggregate supplier data into quarterly or annual CBAM declarations, ensuring traceability of every entry.
+
+
+
+ 2 +
+
Internal Review & Approval
+
Have legal and management teams review reports and sign off on submitted figures before transmission.
+
+
+
+ 3 +
+
Continuous Improvement
+
Record lessons learned, update templates, and schedule training to improve future data quality and compliance readiness.
+
